What is Escrow and How It Protects Freelancers in India

July 11, 2026 — 4 min read

If you've ever been ghosted by a client after delivering work, or paid a freelancer upfront only to receive poor quality — you know the pain. Escrow is the solution that protects both sides.

What is Escrow?

Escrow is a financial arrangement where a third party holds a payment until both sides of a transaction are fulfilled. In freelancing, the client deposits money into escrow before work starts. The freelancer sees the funds are secured and begins working. Once the work is delivered and approved, the payment is released.

Escrow protects freelancers from non-payment and protects clients from incomplete or substandard work. It's the most trusted payment model in online freelancing.

How Escrow Works on Grit&Gigs

Grit&Gigs uses Razorpay-powered escrow for all freelance transactions. Here's the step-by-step flow:

  • 1. Client hires a freelancer — The client selects a gig and places an order.
  • 2. Payment is held in escrow — The client pays upfront, but the money is held securely by Razorpay. The freelancer can see the funds are available.

  • 5. Dispute resolution if needed — If there's a disagreement, Grit&Gigs mediates and decides the fair outcome based on evidence.

Why Indian Freelancers Need Escrow

Indian freelancers face unique challenges. Many clients are small businesses or individuals who prefer paying after work is done — which leaves freelancers vulnerable. Up to 30% of Indian freelancers report being ghosted without payment at least once.

Escrow eliminates this risk. The freelancer knows the money is there before they start. The client knows their funds aren't released until they're satisfied. It builds trust in a market where trust is often the biggest barrier.

Escrow vs Other Payment Methods

  • Bank transfer / UPI — Once sent, you can't get the money back. No protection for either party.
  • Advance payment — Client takes all the risk. If the freelancer disappears, the client loses everything.
  • Post-delivery payment — Freelancer takes all the risk. Client can ghost after receiving work.
  • Escrow — Both parties are protected. Payment is only released when both sides agree the work is complete.
